## Temmuz 2026 — Farming Industry Pain Point x Hasat Vision Correlation

> External research (TZOB, OECD, FAO, saffron-fraud literature) cross-referenced against Hasat's current build (mobile farm journal + verified D2C marketplace, saffron/lavender, Safranbolu/Isparta). Goal: identify which pain point is both most critical and most addressable with what's already shipped, to guide the Ekim–Kasım 2026 saffron season test.

### Stakeholder pain point map

| Stakeholder | Pain point | Criticality (1-5) | Addressable w/ current Hasat product (1-5) | Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Farmer | Intermediary (hal) margin capture — TZOB reports farm-gate→retail gaps >300%; legal hal commission capped at 8% but multi-layered brokerage routinely exceeds it | 5 | 5 | 25 |
| Buyer | Authenticity/provenance risk — saffron is the world's most-adulterated spice (20-30% adulterated globally); buyers can't verify organic/GI claims at point of purchase | 5 | 5 | 25 |
| Farmer | No price transparency — farmer sees hal price only, unaware of D2C/export multiples (Ahmet persona: ₺150/g hal vs ₺400/g D2C) | 4 | 5 | 20 |
| Buyer | Sourcing friction — discovery is phone/WhatsApp, 3-4 week lead times, no searchable verified directory (Zeynep persona) | 4 | 4 | 16 |
| Farmer | No production record-keeping — paper notebook or nothing; blocks certification, financing, trust-building | 3 | 5 | 15 |
| Buyer | Quality/grading inconsistency across small artisanal producers | 3 | 3 | 9 |
| Farmer | Organic/GI certification cost + 3-year conversion + paperwork burden | 3 | 2 | 6 |
| Government | Rural depopulation / aging farmer base — avg. farmer age 58.1, only 1% aged 18-24 (TZOB); framed as a food-security risk | 5 | 1 | 5 |
| Government | TKDK/IPARD subsidy access — ~7 month approval, single Aug-Sep annual window, heavy documentation | 4 | 1 | 4 |
| Farmer | Input/credit financing gap | 3 | 1 | 3 |
| Farmer/Gov | Climate risk & thin crop insurance (TARSIM) for niche crops like saffron | 3 | 1 | 3 |
| Intermediaries (hal, cooperatives) | Cooperatives (Safranbolu Safran Üreticileri Birliği, Isparta Lavanta) lack digital sales tooling, want modernization | 3 | 3 | 9 |

### The core insight: these are the same wedge

Farmer price-capture and buyer authenticity risk aren't two separate pain points — they're the two ends of the same broken link. Hal intermediaries extract 40-60%+ margin *because* there's no verified, direct channel; buyers can't verify origin *because* the chain runs through anonymous, commingled hal lots. A GPS-verified, journal-backed, founder-vouched D2C channel resolves both simultaneously — which is exactly Hasat's core mechanic, not a hypothetical fix. This is why it scores highest on both criticality and addressability at once, and why it's the correct thing to test rather than a distraction from it.

Concretely: this is already what M4-M6 (soft launch → Ekim-Kasım saffron season, %5 take rate, >25% farmer price premium vs hal, >40% buyer repeat rate) is designed to validate. The recommendation isn't "pivot" — it's "instrument the season to prove the wedge," specifically:
- Track price premium captured per transaction vs. hal benchmark (already a KPI) as the farmer-side proof.
- Track whether buyers cite provenance/verification (parcel photos, journal history, GPS) as the reason for premium willingness-to-pay in post-purchase feedback — this is the buyer-side proof and isn't currently instrumented as a discrete signal. Worth adding a 1-question post-purchase prompt ("Bu ürünü seçme sebebiniz?") to close the loop.

### Second-tier addressable — worth keeping, not worth building more for yet
Buyer sourcing friction (WhatsApp/phone → searchable storefront) is already solved by shipped features (storefront, offers, public vitrin URL in P16). No new build needed — it rides on the same wedge above.

Cooperative partnerships (Safranbolu, Isparta) are a distribution unlock already on the TODO — they're farmers with the same core pain point, aggregated, so this is channel strategy, not a new pain point to design for.

### High-criticality but low near-term addressability — correctly out of scope
Rural depopulation/aging farmer base is the pain point governments and TZOB care about most, but no single marketplace app reverses a demographic trend at MVP stage. Hasat's founder-farmer narrative is a legitimate indirect contributor (makes farming visible as a viable modern livelihood to a wider audience) but it's a marketing asset, not a testable product hypothesis — don't build for it, just let the content strategy lean into it.

TKDK/subsidy bureaucracy and input financing are real and severe, but they're Tarfin's and the government's lanes respectively; Hasat is currently a *supplicant* to TKDK (for the farm build), not a solver for other farmers. Flag as a legitimate Phase 2+ expansion (the journal data Hasat already collects is halfway to TKDK/organic-certification documentation for other farmers) but not a wedge to chase now — it would dilute the D2C GTM focus.

### One risk to name explicitly
Hal komisyoncuları are not a pain point to solve — they're an incumbent whose margin Hasat's wedge directly threatens. Expect passive resistance (price undercutting during the Ekim-Kasım window, discouraging farmers from listing) once Hasat has visible GMV in Safranbolu. Not a blocker, but worth a mitigation line in the GTM plan (e.g., locking in farmer commitment before season peak pricing volatility hits).

## Temmuz 2026 — Otomatik Fiyat Toplama Araştırması (Faz 2+, P16-E'ye dokunmuyor)

> Soru: farmer'lardan HAL/Avrupa/manuel fiyat girişi istemek yerine, ileride tüm crop'lar için günlük otomatik fiyat toplama yapılabilir mi? P16-E'nin mevcut MVP kapsamı (manuel küratörlük) değişmiyor — bu sadece ileri faz için kaynak taraması.

### Bulunan kaynaklar (canlı test edildi, 1-2 Temmuz 2026)

| Kaynak | Erişim | Kapsam | Not |
|---|---|---|---|
| **İzmir BB Hal Fiyat API** — `openapi.izmir.bel.tr/api/ibb/halfiyatlari/sebzemeyve/{yyyy-MM-dd}` | ✅ Tam açık, auth yok, JSON, günlük | Sebze/meyve/ithal — ~85 kalem, ortalama/asgari/azami fiyat + birim | Test edildi, gerçek veri döndü (2026-07-01) |
| **hal.gov.tr (Ticaret Bakanlığı HKS)** — Fiyat İstatistikleri + İhracat Fiyat Bülteni | ⚠️ Sadece HTML sayfa (SharePoint, ASP.NET postback, sayfalanmış) — temiz API değil, scraping gerekir | Türkiye geneli sebze/meyve, "Geleneksel/İyi Tarım" ayrımı var | "Servis Geliştirici Kılavuzu" dokümanı var — resmi bir web servisi olabilir, Ticaret Bakanlığı'na kayıt/başvuru ile erişim araştırılmalı |
| **TÜİK Tarım-ÜFE** (data.tuik.gov.tr) | ✅ Açık ama agregat | Aylık üretici fiyat endeksi, ürün bazlı değil | Trend göstergesi olarak kullanılabilir, per-unit fiyat kaynağı değil |
| **EU Agri-food Data Portal** — `api.tech.ec.europa.eu` | ✅ Tam açık, JSON API | 13 AB tarım sektörü (tahıl, süt, et, zeytinyağı, şarap, sebze-meyve, şeker vb.) | "Avrupa" fiyat bacağı için — ama sadece mainstream sektörler |

### Kritik bulgu: hiçbiri safran/lavanta izlemiyor
Hal sistemleri (İzmir API + hal.gov.tr) sebze/meyve; TÜİK agregat endeks; EU portalı 13 mainstream sektör — **hiçbiri baharat/aromatik bitki izlemiyor.** Yani mevcut crop hattımız (safran, lavanta, ileride tıbbi bitki) için otomatik fiyat kaynağı yok ve muhtemelen olmayacak. Manuel küratörlük bir MVP kısayolu değil — niş/özel ürünler için muhtemelen kalıcı çözüm. Otomasyon sadece Hasat ileride hal-tracked mainstream bir ürün eklerse (örn. sebze/meyve kategorisi) karşılığını verir.

### Önerilen mimari (şimdi build etme — kaynak/altyapı notu)
- `price_feed` (P16-E'de tanımlı) tablosuna `source_type` (`manual` | `api`) ekle
- `crop_config` (P16-I) tablosuna opsiyonel `auto_price_source` jsonb ekle (endpoint + ürün-kodu eşlemesi) — safran/lavanta için null kalır
- Günlük job: Supabase `pg_cron` + Edge Function, sadece `auto_price_source` dolu olan crop'lar için otomatik çeker; geri kalanı mevcut manuel curator akışında kalır (aynı UI, farklı `source_type`)
- hal.gov.tr'nin resmi API erişimi (Servis Geliştirici Kılavuzu) için Ticaret Bakanlığı'na başvuru — ayrı bir araştırma/iletişim adımı, teknik değil
